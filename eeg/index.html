EEG Motor Imagery Classification

End-to-end signal processing and classification pipeline on the PhysioNet EEGBCI dataset. Left-hand vs right-hand motor imagery decoded from alpha and beta band power using LDA and SVM, with five interactive figures exploring the signal at every stage.

Overview

During motor imagery — imagining moving a limb without actually moving it — the brain produces a characteristic suppression of alpha (8–13 Hz) and beta (13–30 Hz) oscillations over the contralateral motor cortex. This event-related desynchronisation (ERD) is strong enough to decode left vs right hand intention from scalp EEG alone, forming the basis of non-invasive brain-computer interfaces (BCIs).

This project applies a full signal processing and classification pipeline to the publicly available PhysioNet EEGBCI dataset (subjects 1-10, motor imagination runs), exported as interactive figures so every processing decision can be explored visually.

Preprocessing pipeline

1 Load & concatenate. Three motor-imagery runs (6, 10, 14) loaded via mne.datasets.eegbci and concatenated per subject.
2 Bandpass filter 1–40 Hz (FIR, Hamming window). Low-cut removes slow drift; high-cut excludes EMG and power-line noise above our frequency bands of interest.
3 Average re-reference. Subtracts the instantaneous mean across all 64 electrodes, removing the influence of the physical reference electrode choice.
4 ICA artefact removal (FastICA, 20 components). Ocular components identified by correlation with frontal electrodes Fp1/Fp2 (surrogate EOG) and excluded if correlation exceeds 0.25.
5 Epoching −1 … +4 s around each T1/T2 event, with baseline correction (−1 … 0 s) to remove pre-trial drift.

Signal analysis

Five frequency bands are computed per epoch per channel using Welch's PSD (2-second FFT window, 50% overlap). Alpha and beta are used as classification features; theta and gamma serve as controls.

BandRangeRole in motor imagery
Delta1–4 Hz Baseline control; not directly modulated by imagery
Theta4–8 Hz Working memory and cognitive load; mild modulation
Alpha  C3/C4 8–13 Hz Primary ERD band — suppressed contralateral to the imagined movement
Beta  C3/C4 13–30 Hz Secondary ERD; post-movement beta rebound after imagery ends
Gamma30–40 Hz High-frequency activity; partially overlaps with residual EMG

Classification

Features are the mean alpha + beta band power across all 64 channels (128 features per epoch). Two classifiers are evaluated with stratified 5-fold cross-validation: Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA) and a radial basis function SVM with standard scaling. Results are pooled across all 10 subjects.

Interactive figures

Figure 1 — Raw vs ICA-cleaned EEG signal. Toggle between the unprocessed and ICA-cleaned signal for a 4-second window from subject 1. ICA removal reduces the large slow eye-blink artefact visible in frontal channels (Fp1, Fp2) without distorting the motor cortex signal at C3/C4.

Figure 2 — Power spectral density: left vs right hand imagery. Welch PSD computed across all epochs in each condition (subject 1). The shaded region marks the alpha band (8–13 Hz). Look for the dip in power at C4 during right-hand imagery — alpha suppression contralateral to the imagined movement.

Figure 3 — Alpha band scalp map. Average alpha power per electrode pooled across all 10 subjects. Switch views to compare absolute power in each condition or the ERD% (right minus left, normalised by left). Hover over any electrode to see its name and value. Contralateral alpha suppression appears as a blue patch over C4 during right-hand imagery.

Figure 4 — Event-related desynchronisation (ERD/ERS) spectrogram. Morlet wavelet power in decibels relative to pre-cue baseline (−1…0 s), for subject 1. Blue regions (negative dB) indicate ERD — oscillatory suppression. Switch channels and conditions to see how alpha-beta suppression shifts contralaterally with the imagined hand.

Figure 5 — Classification results. ROC curves for LDA and SVM (left) — hover for the exact TPR/FPR at each threshold. Confusion matrix (centre) for the selected classifier. Per-fold accuracy bar chart (right) shows consistency across the 5-fold CV splits.
