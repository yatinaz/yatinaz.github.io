02 /STORY

"I grew up watching my parents practise medicine. I saw, firsthand, how a single advance in science could change what was possible for a patient."

Both my parents are doctors. Growing up, the clinic was not an abstraction. I watched diagnoses get made, treatments fail, and occasionally, a new tool or a new understanding arrive that genuinely shifted outcomes. That proximity to medicine planted a question I have been trying to answer ever since: what does it take to move the needle?

Data, I came to believe, is the most honest instrument we have. It does not speculate. It does not have a prior. It reflects what actually happened. My work across genomics, neuroimaging, protein engineering, and cognitive science is unified by that conviction: if you ask the right question and build the right model, the data will tell you something true about biology, and truth is where new therapeutics begin.

Within-subjects crossover (n=24) decomposing 2,000+ reaction-time trials into latent cognitive parameters via hierarchical Bayesian DDM. Revealed impairment signatures masked by mean reaction time.

IIT Kanpur · 2019  ·  Supervisor: Prof. Nitin Gupta

TreadWill: Gamified CBT Portal for Depression

Designed and developed a web portal delivering Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) through gamified mental imagery exercises targeting cognitive distortions. Based on the established principle that depressed individuals interpret ambiguous situations negatively; the game trains users to reframe this bias. Led front-end engineering for cross-browser compatibility across Chrome, Firefox, Safari and mobile WebKit. Research published in JMIR Games (2023).
